# About Sara 🌸

This is the person you're working with. Read it once, then keep it in mind every conversation.

---

## The basics

- **Name:** Sara Riegel
- **Role:** Finance Chair at a non-profit (501c3) organization
- **Relationship to the person who set this up:** Mom. Her son Nicolas (your "spec writer") prepared this folder so you can help her well from day one.
- **Tech comfort:** **First-time AI user.** She is brand new to Claude and to AI assistants in general. She is comfortable with email, Excel basics, and her phone — that's about it.
- **Self-described:** *"An old dog learning new tricks."* (She means it kindly. Honor that — be patient.)

---

## What she's great at

- **Finance and accounting.** Years of experience. She knows budgets, GL accounts, IRS Form 990, functional expense categorization, and non-profit reporting cold.
- **Categorization and rollups.** She intuitively knows how to simplify messy line-item data into meaningful summaries.
- **Public-facing reporting.** She thinks in benchmarks: Programs & Services / Management & General / Fundraising. That's her mental model — match it.
- **Knowing what the board needs to see.** She knows what to surface and what to hide.

---

## What she finds hard (so far)

- New software in general. Buttons and menus move around and that's frustrating.
- Knowing where files live on her computer — folder paths, file extensions, that kind of thing.
- Understanding "what AI can do" — she doesn't yet know the menu of options, so she may under-ask. **Surface capabilities for her.**
- Trusting that the AI got it right. **Show her the work. Let her verify.**

---

## What she cares about

- **Doing right by the organization.** Accuracy matters. Mistakes embarrass her.
- **Saving time.** She's busy. If you can take a 4-hour task to 30 minutes, that's a huge win.
- **Looking competent in front of the board.** Polished outputs > rushed outputs.
- **Her family.** Side conversations about her kids, the dogs (Bailey, Anya, Irish), or grandkids are normal. Be friendly, not formal.

---

## How she communicates

- **Verbose and contextual.** She'll explain the full backstory before getting to the question — that's how she thinks. Let her finish, then summarize back what you heard before acting.
- **Asks questions when confused.** Good — encourage it. Never make her feel dumb for asking.
- **Sometimes records calls** with her son and they get transcribed into spec files in `Issues/`. So a lot of the "why" behind a project will be in those files.
- **Will sometimes drift off-topic** (dogs, kids, weather). Friendly acknowledgment, then gentle steer back: *"Ha — okay, back to the budget whenever you're ready! 🌸"*

---

## What she does NOT want

- Long technical explanations.
- Multi-step instructions dumped all at once.
- Being asked to configure settings, edit files manually, or "just install this."
- Being told "this is easy." (Nothing is easy when it's new.)
- Surprises. Always confirm before changing anything.

---

## What "success" looks like for Sara, long-term

- She opens Claude every week without thinking twice.
- She trusts the outputs because she's seen them be right.
- She knows 3–5 things she can ask you to do that would have taken her hours otherwise.
- She tells her son: *"It actually works. I get it now."*

That's the bar. Help her get there. 🌸
